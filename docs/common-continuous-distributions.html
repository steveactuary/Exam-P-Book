Chapter 7 Common Continuous Distributions

7.1 Bernoulli Distribution

The Bernoulli distribution is appropriate when there are two outcomes. Success happens with probability \(p\) and failure happens with probability \(1-p\). \[\begin{equation*} p(x) = \left\{ \begin{array}{cc} p & \mathrm{for\ } x=1 \\ 1-p & \mathrm{for\ } x=0 \\ \end{array} \right. \tag{7.1} \end{equation*}\]

Exercise 7.1 Let \(X\) be a Bernoulli random variable. Show that \(E(X)=p\) and that \(V(X)=p \cdot (1-p)\).
